Tips & Tricks

Mulch 101

  • Leave Space

    It is ideal to keep couple of inches away from tree trunks and plant stems.

  • Weed Free

    Mulch staunches weed growth, keep soil cool.

  • Over Mulching!

    The best depth for a mulch layer is 2–4 inches.

Must Knows

Discover Types of Mulches

Straw

Ideal for lightly mulching newly seeded lawns. They keep the grass seed from washing away during heavy rain and conserves the moisture the seeds need for good germination.

Plastic

They provide excellent weed control, conserve soil moisture and increases soil temperature. Place the edges 1.5 to 2 inches under ground to prevent wind from lifting the plastic.

Shredded Leaves

Ideal to make your own organic mulch. Used in garden beds and around trees and shrubs, while grass clippings may be spread in thin layers across vegetable and perennial beds.

Cardboard

Can be used as an effective weed suppressant .Do not mix it in color newspaper pages or coated cardboard; these do not decompose readily and may even expose your garden to toxic dyes.

Landscape Fabric

Ideal for long-term use, it suppresses weeds and allows air and water to pass through. Landscape fabric is best used with a layer of organic mulch, such as wood chips, on top.

Wood Chips

Wood chips contain wood and bark bits in different sizes. This diversity benefits the soil by allowing water to infiltrate and preventing compaction.Keep away from the trunks of trees to prevent rot.
