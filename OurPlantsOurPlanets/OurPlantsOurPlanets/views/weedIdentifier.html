Most Common Garden Weeds

African Lovegrass

(Eragrostis Curvula)

Native to Southern Africa, the African lovegrass is a vigorous, drought resistant, densely tufted perennial grass that grows to 1.2m with slender, weeping, blue-green to dark green, often in-rolled leaves. The fine seed head is leaden grey and initially long and narrow but opens widely with age.

Horsetail

(Equisetum Arvense)

These are very primitive plants belonging to the genus Equisetum, vascular plants that reproduce by spores in a similar fashion to ferns. These are amongst the list of 28 non-native plants that threaten biodiversity and cause other environ- mental damage. These plants are incredibly invasive, and it's tough to eradicate.

Prickly Pear

(Eragrostis Curvula)

Thick infestations of the prickly pear may impede the growth and regeneration of native vegetation. Native to the Americas, prickly pear is a spiny, drought-resistant succulent that rapidly invades pastures and natural areas and overwhelms other vegetation.

Mexican Feather Grass

(Nassella tenuissima)

Native to North America and South America, These weeds are densely tufted perennial tussock grass. These have been described by weed experts as 'A potential disaster for the Australian environment'. Mexican feather grass which otherwise have been found in rural lands has recently been found growing in private gardens.
